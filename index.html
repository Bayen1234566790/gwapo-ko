CITY OF NAGA TOURIST SPOTS

1. Pasilong sa Naga

Pasilong Sa Naga is a P10.5 million public park designed by world-renowned Cebuano industrial designer Kenneth Cobonpue in collaboration with the local government of Naga, spearheaded by former Mayor Kristine Chiong. Literally means “to find shade in Naga”, Pasilong sa Naga is an architectural park that boasts interlocking rings of elevated walkways, mesmerizing lights, benches, and fountains in the middle of towering acacia trees.

How to Get There?

Going to Pasilong sa Naga is pretty accessible. If you are bringing your own car, you can get directions by using Google Maps or Waze. It’s right across Naga’s St. Francis of Assisi Church and beside Chowking Naga. Make a left turn between Metro Naga and Mercury Drugstore to access parking spaces.

If you are commuting from Cebu City, your best option is to take a KMK bus at SM Cebu City (at the jeepney parking area) and it’ll take you straight to the City of Naga. Just ask the driver to drop you off in front of the church.

2. Boardwalk

The Boardwalk in the city of Naga, Cebu, offers a picturesque blend of coastal charm and urban leisure. As you step onto the wooden planks, the gentle sea breeze carries with it a sense of tranquility, inviting you to explore its scenic beauty.

Stretching along the coastline, the Boardwalk offers stunning panoramic views of the sea, with the distant horizon merging seamlessly with the sky. Whether you visit during the vibrant hues of sunrise or the golden glow of sunset, the vistas are bound to captivate your senses.

The Boardwalk isn't just about the views; it's also a vibrant hub of activity. Families and friends gather here to enjoy leisurely strolls, jogging, or cycling along the well-maintained path. Along the way, you'll encounter locals engaged in friendly banter or fishermen casting their lines into the shimmering waters.

As you wander further, you'll discover quaint pavilions and shaded seating areas where you can pause to take in the surroundings. These spots are perfect for a picnic with loved ones or simply to sit and reflect amidst the serene ambiance.

The Boardwalk also webperves as a cultural hub, hosting events and festivities that celebrate the rich heritage of the region. From traditional music performances to art exhibitions showcasing local talent, there's always something happening to engage visitors of all ages.

For those with a penchant for adventure, the Boardwalk provides access to various water sports and activities. Whether it's kayaking, paddleboarding, or simply dipping your toes in the refreshing waters, there are plenty of opportunities to satisfy your craving for excitement.

As evening descends, the Boardwalk transforms into a romantic retreat, with the gentle glow of street lamps casting a warm ambiance. Couples stroll hand in hand, their laughter mingling with the sound of waves gently lapping against the shore.

Overall, the Boardwalk in the city of Naga, Cebu, is not just a destination; it's an experience that celebrates the beauty of nature, the warmth of community, and the joy of simple pleasures.

How to get there?

Naga City is 45min to 1hr away down south from Cebu City. But may take up to 1.5 to 2 hours trip during rush hours. So if you plan to get there at night, might as well bring with you your extra patience and some food.

For commuters, you have two options:

From Colon Street, ride a jeepney with routes 09C/F, or 10F/H and drop off at Jollibee Bulacao or Pardo Church (P8 fare). From there, take another jeepney with route 44 to reach to Naga Plaza (P15 fare). There are also some 44 jeepneys from/near CIT University in N. Bacalso.

From South Bus Terminal, ride a bus bound for Bato-Oslob or Carcar. Tell the conductor that you will be hopping off at the Naga Church, which is right along the National Highway.

If you’re bring your own car, just navigate via Waze or Google Maps and search for “Naga Plaza”. Naga City is in between Minglanilla (from Cebu City) and San Fernando (from Southern Cebu).

3. Mt. Naupa

Mount Naupa is a stunning natural wonder nestled in the City of Naga, Cebu, Philippines. Rising to an elevation of approximately 584 meters above sea level, it offers adventurers and nature enthusiasts an exhilarating experience with its panoramic views and lush surroundings.

The hike up Mount Naupa is renowned for its moderate difficulty level, making it accessible to both seasoned hikers and beginners alike. The trail is well-established, weaving through verdant forests, meandering streams, and rocky terrain, providing ample opportunities to immerse oneself in the beauty of nature.

Once you reach the summit, you'll be rewarded with breathtaking vistas that stretch as far as the eye can see. On clear days, you can catch sight of the neighboring islands, shimmering blue seas, and the sprawling cityscape below, creating a picturesque backdrop for memorable moments and stunning photographs.

Mount Naupa is not only a haven for outdoor enthusiasts but also a sacred site for locals, with a small chapel located near the summit where visitors can offer prayers and seek solace. Whether you're seeking adventure, tranquility, or simply a break from the hustle and bustle of city life, Mount Naupa promises an unforgettable experience amidst the beauty of nature.

4. St. Francis Xavier Parish Church

The St. Francis Xavier Parish Church, commonly known as the Naga Metropolitan Cathedral, stands as an iconic landmark in the heart of Naga City, Cebu. This historic church is a significant religious and cultural symbol, reflecting the deep-rooted Catholic heritage of the region.

The architecture of the Naga Metropolitan Cathedral is striking, characterized by its Spanish colonial style. The façade features intricate details and ornate decorations, with elements such as arches, columns, and sculptures adorning the exterior. The grand entrance is framed by a towering bell tower, adding to the majestic presence of the church.

Inside, visitors are greeted by a spacious interior adorned with beautiful religious artwork, including paintings, statues, and stained glass windows. The atmosphere is serene and reverent, inviting worshippers and visitors alike to reflect and find solace in the peaceful surroundings.

The church serves as a focal point for religious gatherings and community events, hosting regular masses, ceremonies, and celebrations throughout the year. It is also a popular destination for pilgrims and tourists seeking to explore the spiritual and historical significance of the area.

Surrounded by lush greenery and located amidst the bustling streets of Naga City, the Naga Metropolitan Cathedral stands as a timeless testament to faith, resilience, and the enduring spirit of the Filipino people.

How to get there?

By Private Vehicle:

From Cebu City, head south on N. Bacalso Avenue (also known as the South Road). Continue driving straight for approximately 20 kilometers. Upon reaching the City of Naga, follow the road signs leading to the city center. The Archdiocesan Shrine of St. Francis of Assisi is located near the main city area, so look for local signs or use a navigation app to guide you directly to the church.

By Public Vehicle:

From Cebu City, go to the Cebu South Bus Terminal. Board a bus bound for any of the southern destinations such as Carcar, Dalaguete, or Oslob. Inform the driver or conductor that you will be getting off at the City of Naga. The bus ride typically takes around 45 minutes to an hour, depending on traffic. Once you arrive at Naga, alight at a stop close to the city center or the Naga City Hall. From there, you can take a tricycle or a local jeepney to reach the Archdiocesan Shrine of St. Francis of Assisi.

5. Mangrove forest in Inuboran

The mangrove forest in Inuboran, Naga, Cebu, is a captivating natural wonder teeming with biodiversity and ecological significance. As one of the few remaining mangrove ecosystems in the region, it serves as a vital habitat for various plant and animal species, playing a crucial role in maintaining coastal ecosystems and mitigating the impacts of climate change.

Entering the mangrove forest, visitors are enveloped by a tranquil ambiance, with the gentle rustling of leaves and the occasional chirping of birds creating a harmonious symphony of nature. Towering mangrove trees, with their tangled roots extending above the water's surface, form a dense canopy that provides shelter and nesting grounds for countless marine creatures and migratory birds.

Exploring the winding waterways and narrow channels of the mangrove forest reveals a rich tapestry of life. Mangrove crabs scuttle along the muddy banks, while fiddler crabs perform their intricate mating dances in the shallows. The waters beneath the mangrove roots teem with juvenile fish, seeking refuge from predators and feeding on the abundant nutrients provided by the decomposing organic matter.

For birdwatchers, the mangrove forest is a paradise, offering glimpses of elusive species such as kingfishers, herons, and egrets. With patience and a keen eye, visitors may even spot the majestic sight of an osprey or a brahminy kite soaring overhead, hunting for prey among the mangrove canopy.

Beyond its ecological importance, the mangrove forest in Inuboran serves as a recreational haven for nature enthusiasts and eco-tourists. Guided boat tours and boardwalks allow visitors to immerse themselves in the beauty of the mangrove ecosystem while learning about its significance in coastal conservation efforts.

Preserving and protecting the mangrove forest in Inuboran is essential not only for its ecological value but also for its role in sustaining the livelihoods of local communities and safeguarding the coastal areas against erosion and storm surges. By experiencing and appreciating the wonders of this pristine mangrove ecosystem, visitors can contribute to its conservation and ensure that future generations can continue to marvel at its beauty and diversity.

How to get there?

By Private Vehicle:

From Cebu City, head south on N. Bacalso Avenue (South Road). Continue straight for about 20 kilometers until you reach the City of Naga. Once in Naga, look for signs directing you to Barangay Inoburan. Follow the local road signs or use a GPS navigation app to guide you to the Mangrove Forest in Inoburan.

By Public Vehicle:

From Cebu City, go to the Cebu South Bus Terminal. Board a bus heading towards the southern towns, such as Carcar, Dalaguete, or Oslob. Inform the conductor that you will get off at Inoburan in the City of Naga. The bus ride should take around 45 minutes to an hour, depending on traffic. Upon reaching Barangay Inoburan, you may need to hire a local tricycle or habal-habal (motorcycle taxi) to take you directly to the Mangrove Forest.
